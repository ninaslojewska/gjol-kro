Welcome to Gjøl Kro

Our story and vision

We - myself Peter and my fiancée Natazja - leased the inn back in March 2020, with the vision of making the inn a sort of gathering place for all. A place where groups both large and small can enjoy a meal and also guests who just want to drink a cold beer in the bar area. It's not just for town locals, but also for guests from out of town.

Our services

Our vision is for the inn to once again become an integrated part of the local community, but also an experience guests from all of Nordjylland will want to have. Between the sunroom and the main room, we can house 150 seated quests. We can hold different types of events, including parties, weddings, milestone birthdays, confirmations and much more. Our history

The historical inn dates all the way back to 1851, when it had a thatch roof and served as the town's gathering point. Unfortunately, it burned down around 1900 and was rebuilt the year after, giving it its current form with stepped gables. The gables are easily seen from the Limfjord and can be used as a sort of sea mark that tells sailors that they have arrived in Gjøl. The inn is also where Hans Kirk wrote his novel "The Fishermen" (Danish; Fiskerne), describing the conflict between the residents of Gjøl and a group of newcomer fishermen.
